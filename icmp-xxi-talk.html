Talk at ICMP XXI in Strasbourg

Large deviations of return times and related entropy estimators on shift spaces

We discuss the large deviation principle for several entropy and cross entropy estimators based on return times and waiting times on shift spaces over finite alphabets. We establish precise relations between the rate functions of the different estimators, and between these rate functions and the corresponding pressures, one of which is the Rényi entropy function. For the most commonly used definition of return times, the large-deviation rate function is nonconvex, except in marginal cases. The results apply to shift-invariant probability measures satisfying some decoupling conditions, including irreducible Markov chains, equilibrium measures for Bowen-regular potentials, and invariant Gibbs states for absolutely summable interactions.
